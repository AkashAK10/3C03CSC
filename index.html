FIFA World Cup

The FIFA World Cup, often simply called the  World Cup, is an international football  competition contested by the senior men's national teams of the members of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association ( FIFA), the sport's global governing body. The championship has been awarded every four years since the inaugural tournament in 1930, except in 1942 and 1946 when it was not held because of the  Second World War. The current champion is  France, which won its second title at the 2018 tournament  in Russia.
